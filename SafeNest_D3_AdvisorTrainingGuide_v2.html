Section 01

What Is the D3 Report?

Before you can explain the D3 to a buyer, you need to understand it yourself. Think of it like this:

🏥

Imagine you're going to buy a second-hand car. A normal person just looks at the paint and takes a test drive. But a smart buyer takes it to a mechanic first — the mechanic checks the engine, the brakes, the electrical system, everything. The mechanic gives you a written report. That report is the D3.

The D3 is SafeNest's "mechanic report" for a home.

It checks the project from every angle — the builder's track record, the legal papers, how far construction has reached, how good the flat itself is, and much more. Then it gives the buyer a clear, honest picture so they can decide with confidence.

The D3 is a paid report (₹4,999) or given free when a buyer books an advisory session. It is delivered via WhatsApp as a link. Buyers read it on their phone. It is never printed. It is never emailed as a file.

✓ The D3 IS
  • A deep research report on one specific project
  • Honest — it shows good and bad
  • Based on real documents and site visits
  • Written for the buyer (not the builder)
  • Your main tool in a buyer meeting
✗ The D3 is NOT
  • A brochure or marketing material
  • A guarantee that the project is safe
  • A comparison of multiple projects
  • Something a buyer creates themselves
  • Ever shared by the buyer — only by advisors
Section 02

Your Job as an Advisor

You are the guide. The D3 is the map. Your job is to walk the buyer through the map — not to push them in a direction.

🧭

Think of yourself as a doctor, not a salesperson. A good doctor tells the patient the truth — even if the news is not great. They explain what it means in simple words and help the patient decide what to do next. That's exactly your job with the D3.

You ARE paid by the builder

Yes, your commission comes from the developer. But — and this is important — you earn the same amount no matter which project the buyer picks. You have no reason to push one project over another. This is what makes SafeNest different.

You NEVER recommend blindly

You match the project to the buyer's life — their budget, timeline, whether they plan to live there or rent it out. You say "this project fits your profile because…" not "buy this one, it's the best."

✓ Always do this
  • Read the full D3 before the meeting
  • Know the Watch Items by heart
  • Use the report as your guide — not your script
  • Let the buyer ask questions freely
  • Admit if you don't know something
✗ Never do this
  • Promise things not in the report
  • Dismiss a Watch Item as "not a big deal"
  • Share the D3 link with anyone else
  • Show the buyer the risk numbers (internal use only)
  • Push the buyer to decide on the spot
Section 03

What's Inside a D3?

The D3 has 9 main sections. Each one tells a different part of the story. Here's what each section is and why it matters.

  1. 1
    Vision Block — "Imagine your life here"

    Four sentences that paint a picture of daily life in this project. Not fake marketing language — real, honest description. Example: "Your morning commute to HITEC City takes 25 minutes." This helps the buyer feel the home before they analyse it.

  2. 2
    Basic Facts — "Here's what you're buying"

    Project name, builder name, RERA number, location, type of flat (2BHK, 3BHK, villa), flat sizes, number of floors, and expected handover date. The boring-but-important facts.

  3. 3
    Price & Payment Plan — "Here's what it costs, really"

    The all-in price — not just the brochure price. This includes stamp duty, registration, GST, and any extra charges. Also explains whether the payment plan is CLP (you pay as construction happens) or Subvention (builder pays your loan for some time).

  4. 4
    Quality Assessment — "Here's our score and what drove it"

    The SafeNest Score™ and the Luxury Index™ — explained in plain language. What the builder did well, what was average, and why the numbers are what they are.

  5. 5
    Watch Items — "Here's what to keep an eye on"

    Things that are not necessarily dealbreakers — but things the buyer should know. Always score-triggered, never guessed. Maximum 4 Watch Items per report.

  6. 6
    Suitability — "Is this right for you?"

    A simple table showing whether this project suits someone who wants to live there versus someone who wants to rent it out or sell for profit later.

  7. 7
    FAQ — "Your top questions, answered"

    5–6 questions buyers always ask. Things like "Can I trust this builder?" or "What happens if the project is delayed?" These answers are pre-written — do not change them in the meeting.

  8. 8
    Next Steps — "What do you do now?"

    Maximum 3 action items. Simple, clear instructions for what the buyer should do before making a decision.

  9. 9
    Calculators — "Run the numbers yourself"

    Built-in tools in the report where the buyer can type in their own numbers and see the real cost, EMI, and 4-year financial picture.

Section 04

The SafeNest Score™

The SafeNest Score is a number from 0 to 10. It tells you how good and safe a project is overall. Think of it as the project's final exam result.

📊

Imagine a school report card. Some subjects (like Maths) are more important than others (like Art). So they count for more marks. The SafeNest Score works the same way — each of the 8 things we check is worth a different percentage of the final score.

"This project scored 5.69 out of 10 — we call this 'Conditional.' It doesn't mean the project is bad. It means there are things you need to go in knowing. Let me walk you through what triggered that verdict, and then you can decide if it fits your situation."

Section 05

The Luxury Index™

The Luxury Index tells you what type of product this is — how premium the flat actually is, separate from whether it's safe or not. It's a score from 0 to 100.

🏨

Think of it like hotel star ratings. A 3-star hotel might be perfectly safe and clean. But a 5-star has bigger rooms, a better pool, and fancier fittings. The Luxury Index rates the "star level" of the flat — not whether the builder will deliver it.

The 5 Things That Make Up the Luxury Index

Each "Pillar" is worth up to 20 points. Total = 100 points.

🧱 Pillar 1 — Construction Quality

What material was used to build the walls? Mivan (special aluminium moulds) scores highest. Also looks at ceiling height and how high the ground floor sits above road level (stilt height). Taller ceilings = more premium feeling.

👥 Pillar 2 — Density & Privacy

How many flats per acre of land? How many flats share one floor? How many lifts are there? Fewer families sharing the same lift and corridor means more privacy and a quieter feel.

🏊 Pillar 3 — Amenities

How much amenity space (clubhouse, gym, pool) is there per flat? How much open green space? Does the project have any special premium feature like a sky deck or golf putting green?

📐 Pillar 4 — Spatial Comfort

How big is the smallest flat? How wide are the corridors (the hallways outside your door)? How tall are the doors? Wider corridors and taller doors feel more luxurious.

🚗 Pillar 5 — Infrastructure

How many car parking spots per flat? How many floors in the towers? And how much land is there per flat? More land per flat = more room, greenery, and breathing space.

The 4 Segment Bands

70 – 85LuxuryStrong specs + exclusivity. This is the main segment SafeNest advises on.
45 – 70PremiumBetter than normal market. Good product — but not exceptional in all areas.
≤ 45StandardRegular market specs. Basic amenities. Nothing exceptional.

Section 06

The Risk Score

The Risk Score is an internal number only you can see. Buyers never see the actual number — only the verdict label.

🔄

This score works backwards from what you expect. Normally, 10 out of 10 is the best. Here, 10 means SAFEST and 1 means most risky. So a high Risk Score is a good thing. It's like a safety inspection — 10 means "this car is perfectly safe to drive."

Important: The Risk Score is the average of 7 different checks. Each one looks at something that could go wrong — court cases, RERA delays, the builder's reputation, the land ownership situation, construction progress, whether the money is protected, and past delivery delays.

Risk ScoreVerdictWhat it means in plain words
7.5 – 10.0Low RiskVery few concerns. This project looks clean from a risk perspective.
5.5 – 7.4Moderate RiskSome things to watch. Not alarming, but the buyer should be aware.
3.5 – 5.4High RiskReal concerns. The buyer must fully understand what they're walking into.
0 – 3.4Critical RiskMultiple serious red flags. SafeNest would not normally present this project.

🚫 Never show the buyer the Risk Score number (e.g. "5.57"). Only say the verdict label: "Moderate Risk." The individual sub-scores are for advisor intelligence only.

Say this → Moderate Risk

"Our risk assessment found this project to be at Moderate Risk level. This is not unusual for a project at Foundation Stage with a first-time builder. Let me show you exactly what that means and what protections are in place."

Section 07

Walking Through the Report — Section by Section

Here's how to walk a buyer through each section of the D3 in a real meeting. Use this as your running order.

Start with the Vision Block — always

Before you show any scores or numbers, read the Vision Block out loud. Slowly. Let the buyer picture themselves in the home. This sets an emotional foundation before the analytical discussion. The D3 is designed to help them decide — it starts with heart, then moves to head.

Basic Facts — 2 minutes, quick confirmation

Go through the facts rapidly. Ask: "Does all of this match what you know about the project?" If anything surprises them, stop and clarify. Don't rush past something the buyer questions.

Price & Payment Plan — slow down here

Many buyers have only seen the brochure price. The all-in price is often 15–20% higher. Show them the real number calmly. Don't apologise for it. Say: "This is what it actually costs when you account for everything." Then walk through the payment plan — when do they pay, how much, and when does their loan start?

Quality Assessment — explain the score simply

Show the SafeNest Score and the Luxury Index. Use the simple language from Section 04 and 05 of this guide. For every score, say what drove it — "The amenities scored highest because this project has 150 sqft of clubhouse space per flat, which is excellent."

Watch Items — be matter-of-fact, not scary

Walk through each Watch Item one by one. For each one, say what it is, why it showed up, and what it means for the buyer. Do not say "this is a red flag." Do not downplay it either. Say: "This is something to be aware of. Here is what it means practically."

Suitability Table — personalise this

Look at the table. Then look at the buyer in front of you. If they are an end-user (they want to live there), say "The report shows this project is a good fit for end-users, which matches your situation." If they are an investor, walk through the investor column honestly — including any gaps.

FAQ — read out the questions, not the answers

Ask the buyer "Do any of these questions resonate with you?" Let them pick. Then open the FAQ answer for that question. Don't read through all 5–6 questions if the buyer isn't asking them. Use the FAQ as a reference, not a lecture.

Next Steps — end with clarity

The report gives a maximum of 3 next steps. Read them out. Ask the buyer: "Which of these feels manageable for you in the next week?" Turn the next step into a concrete commitment — "Shall we schedule the site visit for Saturday?"

Section 08

Watch Items Explained

Watch Items are things the report flags for the buyer to be aware of. They are NOT necessarily dealbreakers. They appear automatically based on the scores — not on your personal opinion.

🚦

Think of Watch Items like the warning lights in a car. The oil light doesn't mean your car will explode right now. It means: be aware of this, check it, and deal with it before it becomes a real problem. Same here.

What Triggers a Watch Item

Watch ItemTriggered WhenWhat to say
First-project builderBuilder Track Score < 6"This builder hasn't delivered a project before. That's not unusual, but it means we have no track record to check."
Early construction stageConstruction Score ≤ 4"The project is still in early stages. The possession date is further away, and more can change in that time."
Complex land ownership (JV)JV Score < 7"The builder doesn't own the land — they've partnered with landowners. This adds a layer of complexity."
Money protection concernEscrow Score < 7"We want to see 70%+ of buyer money held in a protected account. This project's escrow setup needs closer attention."
Legal papers concernLegal Score < 7"There are some questions around the land title or legal status. We recommend a lawyer review before booking."
Long handover timelinePossession > 30 months from now"Handover is more than 2.5 years away. That's a long time. Make sure your housing plan works with that timeline."

Rule: Maximum 4 Watch Items in any D3. If more than 4 things are flagged by the scores, we include only the 4 most important. The report is never a list of complaints — it is a focused, honest brief.

Section 09

The Suitability Table

Not every project is right for every buyer. The Suitability Table gives you an honest, structured way to talk about fit.

👓

Think of it like a job interview match. A candidate might be excellent — but if the job needs someone in Bangalore and the candidate only wants to work in Mumbai, it's not a fit. The project quality doesn't change. The fit changes based on the person.

End User ← Wants to live there

Ask: Will they move in? Is the location convenient for their work and family? Does the flat size match their life? Is the possession timeline acceptable? End users care more about how the home feels than how it performs financially.

Investor ← Wants to earn from it

Ask: Is there rental demand in this micro-market? Is the price appreciation potential good? Is the unit size and type something tenants want? Investors care less about ceiling height and more about CAGR and liquidity.

How to Use This in a Meeting
  • Ask the buyer upfront: "Are you planning to live here yourself, or is this an investment?"
  • Once you know — jump to that column in the suitability table
  • Never read both columns as if both are equally relevant
  • If they're unsure — walk both columns and let them see which resonates
Section 10

Hard Questions & What to Say

Buyers will ask tough questions. Some will try to catch you out. Here are the 5 most common hard questions — and exactly what to say.

"You get paid by the builder. Why should I trust you?"

Say this

"That's a fair question, and I'm glad you asked it. Yes, we earn a commission from the developer. But here's the thing — we earn the same amount no matter which project you choose. We have no financial reason to push you towards any one project. Our business runs on you telling your friends and family that we gave you honest advice. If we misled you, that's over. Our interest is in your trust, not your booking."

"The score says Conditional. Should I walk away?"

Say this

"Not necessarily. Conditional means there are things to go in knowing — not that the project is bad. Let me tell you what drove that verdict. Once you understand those specific things, you can decide if they are acceptable for your situation. For some buyers, a Conditional project at a lower price point is the right call. For others, it's not. Let's look at the Watch Items together."

"Builder X told me this project is better than anything else in Kollur."

Say this

"Of course they did — they're selling it. Our job is different. We've scored this project on 8 objective criteria — same criteria we use on every project. The score doesn't have an opinion. Let me show you how this project compares to others in the same micro-market on each of those criteria."

"Can you negotiate the price for me?"

Say this

"We don't negotiate on behalf of buyers — that's not our role. What we can do is help you understand exactly what the price includes, whether it's all-in or not, and what comparable projects have sold for. That knowledge puts you in a much stronger position to have that conversation yourself — or through your legal advisor."

"The builder says 90% is already sold. Is that true?"

Say this

"Builders often share these numbers — and they're not always verified. We score demand and liquidity conservatively. Unless we have confirmed RERA booking numbers, we cap that score at 5 out of 10. In this report, we've reflected what we could verify. I'd encourage you not to make a decision based on urgency created by that claim."

Section 11

What You Can & Can't Share With Buyers

Some information in the D3 is for your eyes only. Other parts are made specifically for buyers. Knowing the difference is critical.

✓ Safe to share with buyers
  • SafeNest Score™ and Quality Verdict
  • Luxury Index™ and Product Segment
  • Risk Verdict (the label — not the number)
  • All project facts (name, RERA, price, size, floor count)
  • Watch Items (the buyer-friendly explanations)
  • Suitability Table
  • FAQ and Next Steps
  • Vision Block and Final Verdict
✗ Never show buyers
  • Internal Risk Score number (e.g., "5.57")
  • Individual risk sub-scores (e.g., Risk_JV_Structure: 4)
  • JV Intelligence section (landowner deals, revenue splits)
  • Scoring Rationale notes (advisor working notes)
  • Litigation Flag details
  • Escrow bank details (internal)
  • Title Type specifics
  • Luxury Index pillar breakdown scores

🚫 Credit rating language is permanently banned. Never use the words "credit rating," "A-rated," "AAA," or any rating agency language. SafeNest is not a rating agency. This is a firm legal and regulatory requirement.

Section 12

The 9-Step Meeting Flow

A typical D3 buyer meeting takes 60–90 minutes. Follow this structure. Don't skip steps — each one builds on the last.

  1. 1
    Welcome & Set Expectations

    "Today I'm going to walk you through our full research on [Project Name]. We'll look at everything — the good, the concerns, the financials. At the end, you'll have a clear picture to make your own decision. No pressure today."

    5 minutes
  2. 2
    Understand the Buyer

    Ask: Are they buying to live there or invest? What's their budget (including all costs)? When do they need the home? Do they have any non-negotiables? This shapes everything that follows.

    10 minutes
  3. 3
    Open the D3 — Vision Block First

    Open the report on screen or hand them the printed version. Read the Vision Block aloud. Let it sink in. Do not rush to the scores.

    5 minutes
  4. 4
    Basic Facts & Price

    Confirm the facts. Walk through the all-in price. Show the payment plan. Let them ask questions here.

    10 minutes
  5. 5
    SafeNest Score™ & Luxury Index™

    Explain the score in simple terms (use the scripts from Section 04 and 05). For each, say what drove it — not just the number.

    15 minutes
  6. 6
    Walk Through Watch Items

    Take each Watch Item one by one. Matter-of-fact. Not alarming. Explain what it is, why it showed up, and what it means practically for them.

    10 minutes
  7. 7
    Suitability & FAQ

    Match the suitability table to their profile (end-user or investor). Then check if any FAQ questions resonate — answer only the ones they care about.

    10 minutes
  8. 8
    Final Verdict & Summary

    Read the Final Verdict from the report. Then give your own one-sentence summary of fit: "Based on what you've told me about your situation, here's how I see this project for you."

    5 minutes
  9. 9
    Next Steps — Concrete Commitment

    Walk through the 3 next steps. Ask which one they'll do first. Lock in a specific action: "Shall we book the site visit for Saturday morning?" End on a clear plan, not a vague "let me know."

    5–10 minutes

Section 14

Non-Negotiable Rules

These are the rules you must never break. Not "guidelines." Not "usually." Never.

  • Never show the Risk Score number to a buyer. Only the verdict label.
  • Never show individual risk sub-scores to a buyer.
  • Never show the JV Intelligence section to a buyer.
  • Never use the words "credit rating," "AAA," "A-rated," or any rating agency language.
  • Never enter data into Airtable formula fields — formulas calculate automatically.
  • Never use RERA portal construction percentages — score construction stage only from physical site evidence.
  • Never fabricate RERA registration numbers, company data, or legal details.
  • Never use alarm language in a report ("red flag," "danger," "stay away").
  • Never recommend one project over another — only show profile match.
  • Never share the D3 link with anyone other than the intended buyer.
  • Never type "N/A" in Premium Feature fields — leave blank if standard.
  • Never enter Corridor Width or Door Height in Premium Feature fields — those have their own fields.
  • Never give Demand/Liquidity Score above 5 without verified booking numbers.
  • Never give Developer Track Score above 5 for a first-project builder.
  • Never promise a buyer something that is not written in the D3 report.
Reference

Glossary of Terms

Every technical word used in SafeNest — explained in the simplest possible language. Alphabetical order.

A
Agreement of Sale (AOS)
The legal paper you sign with the builder when you agree to buy a flat. The possession date is usually counted from this date — e.g., "36 months from AOS."
All-In Price
The real total cost of buying a flat — includes the base price, stamp duty, registration charges, GST, and any other charges. Always higher than the brochure price. SafeNest always shows all-in prices.
Amenity Area
The shared spaces in a project — swimming pool, gym, clubhouse, jogging track, etc. Measured in square feet. The more amenity space per flat, the better.
B
BHK
Bedroom, Hall, Kitchen. A 3BHK flat has 3 bedrooms, 1 hall, and 1 kitchen. A 4BHK has 4 bedrooms, and so on.
Builder / Developer
The company building the project. Sometimes they own the land, sometimes they've partnered with landowners. Not the same as the landowner in a JV project.
C
CAGR
Compound Annual Growth Rate. How much a property's value is expected to grow each year, on average. A CAGR of 12% means the price doubles roughly every 6 years.
Ceiling Height
How tall the room is from floor to ceiling. Measured in feet. 10 ft feels spacious. 9 ft feels normal. 8.5 ft feels low. Premium projects usually have 10 ft or more.
CLP (Construction-Linked Plan)
A payment plan where you pay the builder as the construction progresses. You pay more as each stage is completed — not all upfront. Most common payment plan in Hyderabad.
Commission
The payment SafeNest earns from the developer when a buyer purchases a project through our advisory. Fixed rate — same no matter which project is chosen.
Conditional (Verdict)
A SafeNest Score verdict for projects scoring 5.0–6.4 out of 10. Not necessarily bad — but there are real concerns the buyer must fully understand before proceeding.
Construction Stage
How far along the building work has progressed. SafeNest scores only what an advisor sees on-site — never what the RERA portal says. Five stages: Foundation, Structure WIP <50%, Structure WIP >50%, Finishing, OC.
Corridor Width
The width of the shared hallway outside your flat door. A wider corridor feels more hotel-like. Measured in feet. 8 ft+ is considered good. Has its own field — never put this in Premium Features.
D
D1 Report
The Comparison Report — compares 2–3 shortlisted projects side by side for a buyer. Shorter than the D3, and always free. Delivered via WhatsApp by the advisor.
D3 Report
The Deep Research Report — a full investigation of one project. Priced at ₹4,999 or free with an advisory session. The main tool used in buyer meetings.
Density
How many flats are packed into each acre of land. Fewer flats per acre = more space, privacy, and greenery. The Luxury Index rewards lower density.
Door Height
How tall the main doors in the flat are. 8 ft is standard. 9 ft feels grand. Has its own field in the schema — never put this in Premium Features.
E
EMI
Equated Monthly Instalment. The fixed monthly amount you pay to the bank for your home loan. Calculated based on loan amount, interest rate, and tenure.
End User
A buyer who plans to live in the flat themselves — not rent it out or sell it for profit. End users care more about daily life quality (location, size, amenities) than investment returns.
Escrow Account
A special bank account where buyer payments are held safely — separate from the builder's regular accounts. RERA requires builders to deposit 70% of buyer money into escrow. It protects buyers if the builder runs into financial trouble.
F
Favorable (Verdict)
A SafeNest Score verdict for projects scoring 6.5–7.9 out of 10. A solid, good-quality project with some things to monitor but nothing alarming.
Foundation Stage
The very beginning of construction — digging the ground, laying the base structure. The highest-risk stage because the building is far from complete. Construction Score for this stage: 2 out of 10.
G
GST
Goods and Services Tax. For under-construction flats in India, GST is 5% of the base price. Paid by the buyer on top of the flat price. Not charged on completed (OC-received) projects.
H
Highly Favorable (Verdict)
The top SafeNest Score verdict for projects scoring 8.0 or above out of 10. Strong project with very few concerns. Rare in Hyderabad's market.
HMDA
Hyderabad Metropolitan Development Authority. The government body that approves building layouts and land use in and around Hyderabad. HMDA approval is required before construction can legally start.
I
Investor
A buyer who plans to earn money from the property — either by renting it out or selling it later at a profit. Investors care more about price appreciation potential and rental demand than how the home feels to live in.
J
JV (Joint Venture)
When the builder does not own the land — they've made a deal with the landowners to develop it together and share the revenue. Adds complexity because there are more parties involved. JV details are never shown to buyers.
L
Land Title
The legal proof that the builder has the right to build on this land. A clean title means no court cases, no disputes, no mortgages. An unclear title is a serious red flag.
Lifts Per Tower
How many lifts (elevators) are in each residential tower. More lifts = shorter wait time and more privacy. The Luxury Index calculates how many flats share each lift.
Luxury Index™
SafeNest's 0–100 score for how premium a project's specifications are. Made up of 5 pillars: Construction, Density, Amenities, Spatial Comfort, and Infrastructure. Tells you the "star rating" of the flat — separate from safety.
M
Micro-Market
A specific neighbourhood or area within Hyderabad. SafeNest covers 6 micro-markets: Kokapet, Kollur, Tellapur, Mokila, Narsingi, and Patancheru — all along the ORR corridor in West Hyderabad.
Mivan
A premium construction technique using aluminium moulds instead of traditional brick and concrete. Produces smoother walls, faster construction, and greater structural strength. Always spelled "Mivan" with a capital M in SafeNest's system. Scores 10/10 in the Construction Pillar.
O
OC (Occupancy Certificate)
The government certificate that says a building is safe to live in. Issued after construction is complete and all checks are done. Without OC, you legally cannot move in. Getting OC is the final stage of construction — and when GST stops applying.
Open Space Ratio
The percentage of the project's total land that is open — gardens, parks, walkways — rather than built on. 75%+ is excellent. More open space means a greener, more breathing environment.
ORR
Outer Ring Road — the major highway that encircles Hyderabad. The ORR corridor in West Hyderabad (from Narsingi to Patancheru) is where most of SafeNest's covered projects are located. Good ORR connectivity is a key location factor.
P
Parking Ratio
The number of covered parking spots per flat. A ratio of 1.0 means one parking spot per flat. 1.5 means one and a half spots per flat on average. 2.0 is considered premium. Calculated automatically — never entered manually.
Pillar (Luxury Index)
One of the 5 categories that make up the Luxury Index. Each pillar is worth up to 20 points. The 5 pillars are: Construction Quality, Density & Privacy, Amenities, Spatial Comfort, and Infrastructure.
Pre-EMI Interest
The interest you pay to the bank on your loan during the construction period — before you start paying proper EMIs. With CLP plans, pre-EMI can add significant cost. The D3 calculator shows this clearly.
Product Segment
The category a project falls into based on its Luxury Index score: Standard (≤45), Premium (45–70), Luxury (70–85), or Ultra Luxury (>85). Automatically calculated — never manually assigned.
R
Registration Charges
The government fee to officially register property in your name. In Telangana, total registration cost is 7.5% of the property value (5.5% stamp duty + 1.5% transfer + 0.5% registration). Always included in the all-in price.
RERA
Real Estate Regulatory Authority. The government body that regulates builders and protects buyers. Every project must register with RERA before selling flats. The RERA number lets you verify project details on the official government website.
Risk Score
SafeNest's internal score for how risky a project is. Scored from 0 to 10 — but it works backwards (10 = safest, 0 = most risky). Average of 7 different risk checks. Never shown to buyers as a number.
Risk Verdict
The label version of the Risk Score — what advisors share with buyers. Four levels: Low Risk (≥7.5), Moderate Risk (5.5–7.4), High Risk (3.5–5.4), Critical Risk (<3.5).
S
SafeNest Score™
SafeNest's main project score — a number from 0 to 10. Combines 8 weighted factors (legal, developer, construction, location, product, escrow, JV structure, demand) into a single overall rating. The higher the better.
Stamp Duty
A government tax paid when buying property. In Telangana, stamp duty is 5.5% of the property value. Paid once, at the time of registration. Part of the all-in price calculation.
Stilt Height
How high the ground floor of the building sits above road level. The open ground level (called "stilt") is usually used for parking. Taller stilt = better ventilation, flood protection, and visual grandeur. Measured in feet.
Subvention Plan
A payment plan where the builder pays the interest on your home loan during the construction period. Sounds attractive — but subvention projects are priced 8–12% higher to cover this cost. The D3 calculator shows the real economics.
T
RERA Units
The total number of flats/villas in a project as registered with RERA. Used to calculate density, parking ratio, and amenity space per flat. Always the RERA number — not what the builder claims in their brochure.
TSRERA
Telangana State Real Estate Regulatory Authority — the state-level RERA body for Hyderabad projects. The official source for verifying project registration, approvals, and status.
U
Ultra Luxury
The highest product segment in SafeNest's Luxury Index — projects scoring above 85 out of 100. Exceptional across all 5 pillars. Extremely rare in Hyderabad's current market.
Unfavorable (Verdict)
The lowest SafeNest Score verdict — for projects scoring below 5.0. Multiple serious concerns. SafeNest would not normally present such a project to buyers.
Units Per Floor
How many flats share each floor of a tower. 2 flats per floor is very exclusive. 10+ flats per floor starts to feel like a crowded apartment building. Fewer units per floor = more privacy and quieter corridors.
V
Vision Block
The opening section of every D3 report — four sentences that describe daily life in the project in simple, honest language. Always read aloud at the start of a buyer meeting. Sets the emotional tone before the data.
W
Watch Item
Something in a project that the buyer should be aware of — triggered automatically when a score falls below a threshold. Not necessarily a dealbreaker. Maximum 4 per D3 report. Presented matter-of-factly, never with alarm language.
